Why Create A Kinder World?

You might wonder, "What is the point? The world is going to shit and there isnâ€™t anything we can do to stop it." We hope to show you that the world is not going to hell in a handbasket, that people can make the world a better place to live, and that many people perform small and large acts of kindness towards others each day. We live in this world and our children and grandchildren will continue to live in this world. We need to show them the importance of kindness, and the simple ways in which they can positively contribute to society.

What inspired this project?

Personally, I have seen so many unhappy people in the world. I recently returned from Switzerland, where I studied abroad. I can accurately say that I have seen unhappy people in the United States, Switzerland, and many of the other countries I visited on my journey. As I journeyed through Europe, I often thought to myself, Why are people so unhappy? What makes someone bitter? And I soon began to realize that no matter where I went in the world, unhappy people would always pop up.

But while I was there, I also saw many people who could light up a room when they entered. People who make a difference in the lives of others. People who, even if they didnâ€™t realize it, made the world a bearable place to live. And I thought to myself, What can I do to be more like these positive people? How can I positively impact the world in small or large ways? Thus the inspiration for a different kind of journey. Not a journey from country to country, but rather a journey to a kinder world. An Odyssey where I find myself being a little bit more patient, a little bit more thoughtful, and a little bit more kind.

Spreading some kindness

I woke up on Sunday morning at home. Not my home in Blacksburg, but my childhood home. I had traveled back to Powhatan to spend time with family and friends. I decided that I would stop by and surprise my grandmother before I took the long trek back to the burg.

Now my grandmother is not in the best health. She is 89 years old, has stage 4 dementia, and has been slowly growing weaker over the past few months. I try to make the effort to see her because to be quite honest, for her each day is a blessing.

I walk into the nursing home and down the long hallway, past the dining room where most of the residents are eating their lunch. Of course grandma isn't at lunch;, she barely leaves the room, I thought to myself.

Over the past few months my grandmother has been slowly isolating herself from the other residents. She barely goes out for meals, and never attends the scheduled activities. I can only blame her so much though. I mean, the woman is 89 and the majority of the time she doesn't feel well. But it has been sad to see her steep decline.

I walk up to her bedroom door and walk inside. As soon as I turned the corner and she saw me, I could see her entire disposition change. She went from slumped in the chair to bright eyed and smiling in 2.0 seconds. "Emily!" she says "Am I glad to see you! What a surprise!" I immediately go over and give her a hug.

After spending a few minutes talking to her about what is going on in my life, we began to talk about her life. She confides in me that she hasn't been feeling well lately, but "Boy am I glad you are here!"

She always manages to thank me at least 5 times for visiting before I leave. I know that she appreciates me coming, but I think today more than usual. You never know what someone is going through, and after hearing how my grandmother wasn't having a good day but that when I came she "perked up" means everything to me.

My grandmother always tells me how my visits make her day. Visiting my grandmother isn't difficult for me. It only takes a few gallons of gas, and my willingness to tell her about what is going on in my life. But it is something that makes all of the difference to her.

Kindness doesn't have to be extravagant. It doesn't have to be a thoroughly planned out event, or some grand gesture. It could be as simple as a visit with your elderly grandmother.

I challenge you to embark on this journey to kindness with me. Help me, help yourself, and help the world around you to become a better place.
